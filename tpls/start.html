Description

You are invited to participate in a research study conducted by the investigators listed above. The purpose of this study is to investigate your preferences in gameful applications and your enjoyment of certain gameful applications. If you decide to volunteer, you will be asked to fill out an online survey and to interact with two different gameful applications. Overall, the online study should take approximately 15 minutes. First, you will be asked to provide demographic data including your age and gender. Afterwards, a questionnaire to assess your preferences in gameful applications follows. After answering questions about the perception of filling out this questionnaire, you will be asked to interact with two gameful applications. In one application, your task is to collect as many virtual items as possible by clicking on one of two statements, depending on which statement is more important to you. In another application your goal is to shoot snowballs at targets unlocking virtual items in order to make progress towards different goals within the gameful application. After interacting with each gameful application, you will be asked to answer questions about your perception and enjoyment of these applications. In both applications, your input (i.e. mouse clicks) is stored.

Participation in this study is voluntary. You may skip any question you prefer not to answer. You can withdraw your participation at any time without penalty or loss of remuneration by clicking through to the end of the survey. To receive remuneration, please proceed to the end of the questionnaire and click on the link to get back to Prolific. All incomplete data records will be deleted by us. After the end of data collection, data can no longer be withdrawn, as the researchers will not know which data came from which participant. You will receive 2.00 pounds for taking participation this study. There are no immediate benefits for you. Your participation contributes to game-related research and might help to improve the user experience in gameful applications. Please note that interacting with the gameful applications might cause minor psychological distress, such as minor stress due to the timed nature of the gameful applications.

It is important for you to know that participation is anonymous in that we do not ask for your name. All the data will be summarized for publication and no individual could be identified from these summarized results. This study is being conducted as part of Maximilian Altmeyer's PhD dissertation.

You will be completing the study via an online survey operated by the researchers on a web server operated by netcup (https://www.netcup.eu/). When information is transmitted or stored over the internet privacy cannot be guaranteed. There is always a risk your responses may be intercepted by a third party (e.g., government agencies, hackers). The researchers will not collect machine identifiers such as IP addresses. Netcup may temporarily collect this information without our knowledge and make this accessible to us but will not collect information that could identify you personally. We will not use or save this information without your consent.

The data with no personal identifiers collected from this study will be maintained on a password-protected computer database in a restricted access area of the university. As well, the data will be electronically archived after completion of the study and maintained for at least seven years and then erased. To analyze the data, the anonymized data will be saved on Maximilian Altmeyer's secure personal computer. The anonymous dataset will also be made publicly available on https://osf.io/ (Open Science Foundation).

For all other questions, contact Maximilian Altmeyer (maximilian.altmeyer@gmail.com), Dr. Gustavo Tondello (gustavo@tondello.com) or Dr. Lennart Nacke (len@uwaterloo.ca).
